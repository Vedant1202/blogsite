Vidyalankar Institute of Technology achieves NAAC Accreditation with A+ Grade

Vidyalankar Institute of Technology, an Engineering and Management Institute centrally located in Mumbai, has achieved accreditation with an A+ Grade by National Assessment and Accreditation Council (NAAC). Among all categories of institutes accredited A+ in the First Cycle as per the Revised Accreditation Framework, Vidyalankar Institute of Technology, with a score of 3.41 stands at First position in Maharashtra and Second in India.
VIT aims to create industry-ready professionals, research scholars and proficient entrepreneurs by infusing the right blend of toolsetwhich includes award-winning world class infrastructure and technological expertise, skillset with focus on honing technical and soft skills and mindset which inculcates professional acumen and social sensitivity in academics and sensitizes students towards addressing societal needs-. The institute encourages innovative thinking and intellectually stimulates them towards technology and research.
Besides academics with strategic delivery mechanisms in place, the focus of the Institute is also on cocurricular and extra-curricularactivities which bring improvement in academics and life skills. To be categorized ‘ A+’ by NAAC is a significant milestone in the journey of success for VIT. The Institute has now set its targets on academic autonomy, and faculty and student exchange programs at an international level.

Swaralankar @ Vidyalankar: A Celebration of Classical Melodies

Music gives a soul to the universe,wings to the mind, flight to the imagination and life to everything.” Vidyalankar Polytechnic initiated an attempt in the form of ‘SwaralankarSolo Light Music Competition’, to foster and value music both as part of a universal education, and as an essential expression of the human experience and spirit.
The event witnessed a warm response on 11 February 2019 by proudly hosting more than 100 singers from all over Maharashtra. To embellish the musical experience in a significant way, VP invited ace judges of the music fraternity. The Chief Guests Shri AnandBhate and Shri Kaushal Inamdar also enjoy a substantial stature in the field of classical music.
The winner was awarded with Rs.10000/- along with 2 Days 1 Night stay at Mahabaleshwar sponsored by Anandvan Bhuvan. In today’s scenario, many educational institutes are engrossed in grade based learning, promoting mechanical education. This activity organised and accomplished by VP is a remarkable step that fosters opportunities for cultural and aesthetic experiences of participants through active participation in music, and helps to enhance their persona.

The Ripple Effect: NSS-VIT making a difference!

The National Service Scheme (NSS) is a community service program involving youth on a very large scale to participate in community service. The sole aim of the NSS is to provide hands-on experience to young students in delivering community service without any bias.
The students of Vidyalankar Institute of Technology led by the Program Officer Prof. Pravin Gharge and a team of dedicated teachers have successfully revived NSS in the campus and are emerging as a strong unit for community service. The NSS-VIT unit provided a helping hand for the ‘Kerala Flood Victims’ by organizing a donation drive on campus and transporting around 30-35 cartons of flood relief material to the flood affected areas in Kerala.
After Ganesh Visarjan, the NSS-VIT volunteers put in coordinated efforts to cleanup the Dadar Chowpatty beach by sorting out plastic and other non-biodegradable waste. This idea of beach clean-up is the smallest act of kindness that is worth more than the grandest intention.
The NSS volunteers at Vidyalankar Institute of Technology believe in the philosophy that “I am only one, but I am one. I cannot do everything but I can do something. And I will not let what I cannot do interfere with what I can do”. Happiest are the NSS volunteers at VIT who lose themselves in the service of the others!
